# Rooms and Device Placement

A detached single-family house in Germany, occupied by Lena, Tom and their
14-year-old daughter Mia. Each smart-home device lives in exactly one room. A
resident can only interact with a device when in the corresponding room (or when
passing through).

## Bedroom (Lena & Tom)
The parents' bedroom.
- `bedroom_lamp` — ceiling/bedside bulb for getting up in the dark and for the evening.

## Mia's Room
Mia's bedroom and study space.
- `mia_lamp` — bulb for getting up, homework and winding down.

## Living Area
Open-plan living room where the family spends shared time.
- `living_lamp` — main ceiling light for the living area.
- `tv_socket` — smart plug powering the TV.
- `robot_vacuum` — robot vacuum for the living area / ground floor.
- `heating` — relay for the living-area electric heater.

## Kitchen
Kitchen, opening onto the living area.
- `kitchen_lamp` — kitchen ceiling light.
- `coffee_socket` — smart plug for the coffee machine.

## Bathroom
Shared family bathroom.
- `bathroom_lamp` — bathroom light.

## Hallway / Entrance
- `hallway_lamp` — hallway light, used briefly when entering or leaving the house.
